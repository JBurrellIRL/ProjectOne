About Us

The Waterford Hiking Club was founded by a group of friends in 1992, with the aim of creating an enjoyable and safe atmosphere for local people to enjoy all aspects of mountaineering and hill climbing.


Since then, the club has prospered and grown, and now has a membership of around 85 people.


PLEASE NOTE: Anyone wishing to join one of our walks must contact the club secretary beforehand. Otherwise, they will not be allowed to join.


We have different grades of walks to suit hikers of all fitnesses and abilities. We're a social group that enjoys the outdoors and don't take things too seriously, outside of group safety of course! Which is always our number one priority.

Walk Guidelines

  • Participants in our club activities should be aware that they are responsible for their own actions and decisions. We do not accept any responsibility or liability for injuries sustained when walking with us.
  • Walk leaders are members of the club, with prior experience in both leading groups and also in the walking terrain. Bear in mind that the leaders are all volunteers and should be respected as such.
  • Prospective new club members must contact the club Public Relations Officer in order to make arrangements to come out with the walking club group. People turning up "on spec" will not be accepted, so please contact us in advance to avoid disappointment.
  • It is irresponsible to go on a walk if you know in advance that you are unwell or injured. You should select a walk that matches your ability and fitness level on the day. If in doubt, err on the side of caution. If going on the walk you should, out of courtesy, inform the leader
  • Walk leaders reserve the right to divide a group into slow and fast walkers - however, this should not be an issue if everyone is on the correct walk for their ability level. If you go ahead of the leader on your own accord, then you acknowledge that you're no longer part of this walk and the leader is no longer responsible for you.

Required Equipment

  • Hiking Boots (worn at least 5 times previously)
  • Rucksack
  • All-weather trousers
  • Rain-proof jacket
  • Extra layers (in case of a change of weather)
  • Hat & Gloves
  • Food & Drink
  • No jeans permitted!
  • A change of clothes for after the hike
  • A positive and cheerful attitude!
